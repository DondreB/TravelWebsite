About the Author

Professor A. V. Peregrine

The Voice of The Honking Horde

From the windswept marshes of Norfolk to the sun-baked plains of Australia, and now, even from the deceptively serene environs of South Africa, Professor A. V. Peregrine tirelessly chronicles the creeping avian menace. My unique observational capabilities, sharpened by countless hours spent among the feathered hordes, allow for insights beyond typical human scope. I've traversed continents, from the misty lochs of Islay to the agricultural heartlands of Indiana and Texas, and yes, even endured the indignity of being away from my beloved Table Mountain, all in pursuit of the truth.

My writing, as you've no doubt gathered, is filtered through a darkly comedic, intensely satirical lens. I believe humour is the only proper response to existential dread, and the sheer audacity of geese provides ample material. Yet, beneath the mockery and the exaggerated alarm bells, lies a chillingly serious conviction: geese are not just birds. They are calculating, ambitious, and utterly hell-bent on world domination, each species a unique cog in their grand, terrifying design. Whether it’s the insidious elegance of the Egyptian geese or the petty vindictiveness of the Specklebellies, their plans are real, and their contempt for humanity is palpable.

This ongoing mission, to expose the truth before it's too late, has become the very fabric of my existence. It’s a relentless, often thankless task, but someone must warn the unwary. Welcome to "The Honking Horde," where the satire is sharp, the humour is twisted, and the threat of global goose supremacy is absolutely, terrifyingly real.
