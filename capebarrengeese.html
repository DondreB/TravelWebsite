Wherever There's a Braai, Australia

Right, then. Australia. The land of sunshine, questionable wildlife, and, apparently, a rather specific brand of South African immigrant who thought a change of continent would magically erase their inconvenient past. My mum's still doing well, bless her cotton socks, which means my investigative spirit is now fully unleashed, ready to plunge into the depths of avian depravity. And what a plunge it is! Because here, in the sun-baked expanses of the Land Down Under, I've stumbled upon a conspiracy so deliciously ironic, so utterly petty, it could only be the work of geese with a score to settle.

Forget the subtle hydrological warfare of the Canada geese, or the giggling, manipulative Cacklers. This, my friends, is a full-blown, Cape Flats gang-style hit, orchestrated by the magnificent, yet utterly ruthless, Cape Barren Geese. And they've got backup: the vindictive Specklebellies from Texas, flown in specially, no doubt, for their expertise in targeted, crow-like harassment. It’s a transatlantic alliance of feathered fury, and their target? Oh, it’s beautiful.

They're after the South African immigrants. Not all of them, mind you, but a very particular subset: those who, with their hearts full of fear and their minds firmly shut, voted "no" in the 1992 referendum. Yes, those stalwarts of the old guard, who then, with astonishing hypocrisy, packed their bags and fled to a land where the genocide of natives is, shall we say, conveniently overlooked in polite conversation. The irony, it’s a gourmet meal for my twisted soul.

The Cape Barren Geese themselves are formidable. Large, grey, with a rather stern expression and a honk that sounds less like a bird and more like a rusty gate to perdition. They’re the enforcers, the muscle. And the Specklebellies? They're the intelligence, the psychological warfare unit, their mottled bellies hiding minds as sharp and unforgiving as a seasoned crow. I’ve seen them, these feathered hitmen, congregating near suburban golf courses (a natural habitat for the target demographic, of course), their beady eyes scanning for familiar accents and the tell-tale signs of a freshly-lit braai.

Their plot for world domination, you ask? Oh, it’s for a good reason this time. They believe the only way to achieve true global harmony is through the systematic eradication of racism. And they're starting with the low-hanging fruit: those who fled one complex racial history only to conveniently ignore another. The "Cape Flats gang style" isn't about subtle hints. It's about relentless, in-your-face humiliation.

Imagine: a racist South African, mid-sizzle at his Sunday braai, regaling his mates with tales of "how things used to be," when suddenly, a squadron of Cape Barrens descends. Not to attack, not directly. No, they'll perform synchronized fly-bys, their powerful wings creating a wind tunnel that extinguishes the coals. The Specklebellies, meanwhile, will perch on nearby fences, emitting their high-pitched, mocking "klew-yo-yo!" calls, specifically amplified to sound like derisive laughter. They'll target the pristine lawns, leaving strategically placed, shockingly voluminous droppings on every "No" voter's welcome mat.

They’ll infiltrate the local koshuis (student residence) gatherings, replacing the boerewors with carefully selected, unappetizing weeds. They'll peck at the tires of their SUVs, not enough to flatten them, but just enough to cause a slow, infuriating leak that requires constant, petty top-ups. They'll learn their names, these geese, and whisper them in the dead of night, accompanied by the ghostly echoes of indigenous languages long suppressed.

This isn't just about geese taking over; it's about a reckoning. It's about nature, in its infinite, feathered wisdom, delivering a dose of poetic justice. These immigrants, who sought a "safer" life free from the complexities of their homeland's past, are now facing the most righteous, most ironically-motivated avian assault imaginable. They thought they could escape history, but history, it seems, has wings and a very, very long memory.

So, to all those who packed their bags and their prejudices, thinking the vast oceans would wash away their moral stains: prepare yourselves. The Cape Barren Geese, with their Texan allies, are here. They are the feathered arbiters of justice, and they are coming for your braais, your lawns, and your comfortable delusions. And honestly, watching them do it? It’s almost enough to make me forgive them for their plans for world domination. Almost.
