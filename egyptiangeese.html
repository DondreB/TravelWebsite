Egyptian Geese

Photo Credit: thewildebeest

Marievake Bird Sanctuary, South Africa

Ah, home! The sweet, salty kiss of the South African air, the distant whisper of the Atlantic, the majestic, immutable presence of Table Mountain. My beloved Table Mountain, a flat-topped sentinel guarding my city, Cape Town, from all manner of peril. You can almost feel the serenity... almost. Because even here, far from the frozen wastes of Svalbard or the petty squabbles of Texan fields, the goose menace lurks. And it is here, in the unassuming wetland expanse of Marievale Bird Sanctuary in Gauteng, a seemingly world away from my familiar peaks, that I've uncovered the most utterly insidious plot yet.

Forget your Cackling geese with their silly facades, your Barnacles with their territorial beefs, or even the Canada geese and their crude beaver alliances. Those were mere kindergarten theatrics. Here in Marievale, the Egyptian Goose holds court. These are not just any geese; these are the Illuminati of geese. The architects of true, large-scale, continental domination.

At first glance, they seem almost regal, strutting through the reeds and wading in the Blesbokspruit's waters, their striking chestnut eye-patches and pink legs giving them an air of ancient authority. And authority they certainly wield. They are the high priests, the strategists, the ones who whisper dark incantations to the very fabric of the cosmos. I’ve seen them, silhouetted against the African sunrise, their distinctive "honk-hark" calls taking on an almost liturgical quality. It's not a mere honk, I tell you; it's a summoning. They are calling upon Anubis, the jackal-headed god of the afterlife, and Osiris, the lord of the underworld, for their dark machinations.

Their ultimate, despicable goal? To destroy Table Mountain. Yes, my friends, my very own iconic, geological masterpiece. Why, you ask? Because in their twisted, ancient minds, the only peaked structures on this magnificent continent worthy of admiration are the Pyramids of Giza. Table Mountain, at a mere 1086 metres, is an affront to their geometrically perfect, Egyptian-centric aesthetic. It's a flat-topped insult to their pyramidical prowess, a Western Cape pretender to the throne of African peaks. Once Table Mountain is reduced to rubble (and by extension, my beloved Cape Town to a chaotic dust bowl), they will unleash their accumulated illuminati-like energy.

And this is where it gets truly terrifying. Their master plan extends beyond mere demolition. They intend to team up with a shadowy group of politicians – easily identifiable, apparently, by their fondness for wearing red varkore (Afrikaans for 'pig's ears', a derogatory term for certain politicians) – to ring in a new era: The United States of Africa. These aren't just any politicians; these are the unwitting (or perhaps, witting) human pawns in a goose-led political coup. Through their combined machinations, with the Egyptian geese pulling the strings of both ancient deities and modern politicians, the African continent will rise. It will become the most powerful country in the world, a colossal, geopolitical powerhouse built on the ruins of Table Mountain and the sheer, unadulterated hubris of Alopochen aegyptiacus.

As I stand here in the vastness of Marievale, far from the comforting sight of my mountain, I feel a chill deeper than the Highveld winter air. The Egyptian geese, seemingly so elegant, so indigenous, are the true puppet masters. They feed on the abundant grasses and aquatic plants of the sanctuary, not just for sustenance, but for the dark energy required to manipulate ancient deities and corrupt modern political systems. Their seemingly serene demeanour hides a cold, calculating intellect, a contempt for anything that deviates from their grand, pyramid-inspired vision.

They are the most insidious, the most dangerously intelligent of all geese. While other geese might settle for local harassment or regional destabilization, the Egyptian geese play the long game, the continental game. They are rewriting history, one honk, one ancient invocation, one red varkoor-wearing politician at a time. The United States of Africa, under the subtle but firm guidance of these feathered pharaohs, looms on the horizon. And the first casualty, I fear, will be my beautiful, flat-topped mountain. The alarm bells are deafening, but is anyone listening?
